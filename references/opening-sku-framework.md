# Opening SKU Framework: 已认 vs 未认

Distilled from session `888d56ee` (四月开业牛排选品, 2026-03-14).

## The Core Insight

Hard discount retail creates a specific form of cognitive dissonance:
**"This store is supposed to be cheap. But that product is supposed to be expensive."**

The opening pre-sale is the ONE moment to weaponize this dissonance. After opening,
customers calibrate. Before opening, everything is discovery. Use it.

---

## Framework: Two Paths to "Explosive Product"

### Path A: 大家已认的商品 + 价格跌破心理预期

**Mechanism**: Consumer has a pre-existing price anchor from normal retail.
Your price shatters that anchor. The gap is the product.

**Trigger phrase** (what customer says to friends):
> "你猜我在那家新店买了什么？M5 和牛，才 69 块。对，就是那种几百块的东西。"

**Selection criteria**:
- Product must be recognizable by name OR by appearance (no explanation needed)
- Anchor price must be widely known (盒马/山姆/淘宝bestseller price)
- Your price should be >40% below anchor to trigger the "impossible" reaction
- Quality must be verifiable at point of purchase (e.g., marbling grade visible through packaging)

**Common Type A archetypes**:
- Premium proteins with visible quality signals (wagyu, salmon, lobster)
- Name-brand packaged goods (Japanese snacks, Korean beauty, European dairy)
- Seasonal luxury items (hairy crab, king crab, premium fruit)

**Risk**: Suppliers may limit volume at your price point. Validate availability before marketing.

---

### Path B: 大家不认识的商品 + 价格公道 + 体验惊艳

**Mechanism**: Consumer has NO price anchor. There is nothing to compare against.
First experience sets the reference point. If it's great, you win forever.

**Trigger phrase** (what customer says to friends):
> "不知道为什么这个叫板腱，但是真的好吃，而且才 60 块，下次还要买。"

**Selection criteria**:
- Product must be genuinely unfamiliar to the TARGET consumer (not just to the researcher)
- Name can be unfamiliar — but appearance/description must be enticing on its own terms
- Price must be "obviously fair" not "suspiciously cheap" (too cheap = quality doubt)
- First-bite experience must reliably surprise upward (test this before committing)

**Common Type B archetypes**:
- Secondary cuts with superior experience (wagyu flat iron, beef cheeks, bavette)
- Regional specialty foods not yet mainstream (specific regional varieties, heritage breeds)
- Imported items with no equivalent in domestic market
- Novel formats of familiar ingredients (e.g., ultra-thin wagyu slices, single-serve premium packs)

**Risk**: Requires more in-store education. Consider tasting stations or QR → recipe video.

---

## The Combination Rule

A strong opening lineup needs BOTH types. Suggested ratio:
- **2-3 Type A** (traffic drivers — people come because they've heard about the price)
- **2-3 Type B** (margin drivers + loyalty seeds — people come back because they discovered something)

**Do NOT build an opening around only Type A**. Shocking prices draw foot traffic once.
Discovery products create repeat customers.

**Do NOT build around only Type B**. Unknown products don't spread before the store opens.
You need at least one "anchor SKU" that travels by word of mouth.

---

## SKU Brief Template

For each candidate, fill this out before scoring:

```
SKU: [specific product name + grade + spec, e.g., "M5 板腱牛排 200g"]
Type: A / B
Type A anchor: [who sells it, at what price, where consumer sees it]
Type B discovery: [why unknown, what the experience delivers, why price feels right]
Viral hook: [one sentence a customer would actually say to a friend]
Cooking barrier: [none / low (microwave/pan) / medium (some skill) / high (technique required)]
Visual quality signal: [what the customer can see to verify quality before buying]
```

---

## Red Lines

1. **Never use vague SKU definitions** in the output. "和牛" is not a SKU. "M5 板腱 200g ¥59.9" is.
2. **Never fake Type A** — if consumer doesn't know the anchor price, it's Type B. Be honest.
3. **Never recommend Type B when the product requires explanation at point of sale** — if it
   needs a 30-second pitch to make sense, hard discount floor staff won't deliver it consistently.
4. **Opening lineup must be testable in advance** — if you can't verify the product exists at
   the claimed price from a reliable supplier before the opening, don't put it in the lineup.
